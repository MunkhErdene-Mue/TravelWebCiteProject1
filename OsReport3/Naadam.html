NAADAM

Naadam is a traditional festival in Mongolia that celebrates the "Three Manly Games" or "Three Manly Sports."
It's a significant cultural event in Mongolia and typically takes place in July.

Three Manly Games:

Naadam features three main traditional sports, which are considered the epitome of manhood in Mongolian culture.

Three Manly Games

Wrestling:

Wrestling is a highlight of Naadam. Competitors wear traditional tight-fitting, open-chested costumes, and the goal is to throw the opponent to the ground. Wrestlers aim for a series of victories to reach the championship rounds.

Rules:

The objective of Mongolian wrestling is to throw your opponent to the ground, making any part of their body, except their feet, touch the ground.

Horse Racing:

Child Jockeys: One of the most distinctive features of Mongolian horse racing is that the jockeys are typically children, often as young as 5 or 6 years old. These young riders display remarkable riding skills and endurance, making them an integral part of the races.

Distances:

Unlike many other forms of horse racing that take place on oval tracks, Mongolian horse races are conducted on open terrain, often covering long distances. Races can range from 15 to 30 kilometers or more, depending on the age and category of the horses.Although the races are long-distance, they require strategy and skill.

Archery:

the target distance is quite far, typically around 75 to 90 meters away from the archers.

The targets used in Mongolian archery are small leather cylinders, about the size of a soda can, placed on top of wooden stands. These targets are lined up in a row along the shooting range.

Shooting from Horseback

Archers participate while riding on horseback, which makes Mongolian archery unique and challenging.They shoot at the targets while their horses are moving at a moderate pace, often at a canter or gallop.

Archers accumulate points for each successfully hit target, and the one with the most points after multiple rounds wins the competition. It's a challenging and culturally significant sport that showcases both precision and horsemanship skills.

Naadam typically begins with a grand opening ceremony, featuring parades, traditional music, and dances. The event often kicks off with a colorful procession of participants and dignitaries.

Symbolism

the entire country takes part in the celebrations.

Naadam embodies the resilience, strength, and skills required to thrive in Mongolia's challenging environment. It reflects the nomadic heritage of the Mongolian people and their enduring connection to their land and horses..
